Types of Anxious Thoughts

We are all familiar with basic types of anxious thinking, such as:

  • worrying (I won't fit in),
  • having anxious memories (It's just like that awful speech I gave in seventh grade),
  • evaluating or judging (This isn't going very well, You look like an idiot), and
  • making negative comparisons (He's way more interesting than I am).

Other, slightly more elaborate types of thinking the socially anxious mind favors include the following.

Fortune-Telling: When your mind predicts what will happen, usually something negative

Example: I won't have anything to say, I'll have a panic attack and won't be able to speak, and everyone will discover that I'm incompetent.

Mind Reading: When your mind tells you what others are thinking about you, usually in the form of judgmental thoughts.

Example: thoughts starting with He thinks..., She thinks..., or They think.. (for example, She must think I'm a nervous wreck or They think I'm stupid and boring).

"Shoulding": Using statements that include the word "should," is a popular activity of the socially anxious mind.
